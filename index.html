Welcome to Kerala: God's Own Country

Kerala, located on the southwestern coast of India, is often referred to as "God's Own Country" for its breathtaking landscapes and rich cultural heritage. The state boasts serene backwaters, lush tea gardens in Munnar, and pristine beaches that invite relaxation and exploration. Visitors can enjoy the tranquil waters of Alleppey on traditional houseboats, drifting through interconnected lakes and rivers while witnessing the peaceful rhythms of life along the banks.

In addition to its natural beauty, Kerala is a cultural treasure trove. The state is renowned for its classical dance forms, such as Kathakali and Mohiniyattam, which bring ancient stories to life through intricate movements and vibrant costumes. Keralaâ€™s colorful festivals, like Onam and Vishu, showcase its rich traditions and customs. The local cuisine, featuring coconut, spices, and fresh seafood, tantalizes the taste buds with dishes like Malabar biryani and Payasam. With its unique blend of adventure and tranquility, Kerala promises an unforgettable experience for every traveler.

Where to Go

Explore the must-visit destinations in Kerala, each offering a unique experience.

Alleppey

Known for its backwaters and houseboats, Alleppey is a paradise for nature lovers.

Munnar

Famous for its tea plantations, Munnar offers breathtaking views and pleasant weather.

Varkala

A beautiful beach destination with cliffs and natural springs, perfect for relaxation.

Jadayu Center

A unique adventure park showcasing the legend of Jatayu from the Ramayana.

Fort Kochi

Rich in history and culture, known for colonial architecture and art galleries. With beautiful food varieties.

Thekkady

Home to the Periyar Wildlife Sanctuary, offering wildlife spotting and spice tours.

Wayanad

Famous for its lush green hills and waterfalls, Wayanad is perfect for trekking.

Kumarakom

Located on the banks of Vembanad Lake, known for its backwaters and bird sanctuary.
